水時敘事——淹沒故土的紀念裝置

Preface · 序

2021 年的旱災，家鄉的鯉魚潭水庫水下的遺址開始頻繁出沒。做為一個印象深刻的契機，我開始找尋鯉魚潭水庫背後口耳相傳的故事……

The drought in 2021, underwater ruins at the Liyutan Reservoir in my hometown began to appear frequently. As a memorable opportunity, I began searching for the oral stories behind the Liyutan Reservoir...

水庫的發展雖成就了萬畝農田，支持台中與苗栗的人口與產業發展，但不可否認的是背後的犧牲：消失的村落與這片土地的故事。如今只能透過新開村居民及外移居民的打聽、民間記錄文本的調查、客家委員會官方資料等文資，才可拼湊出過去這片土地上居民們生活的全貌。

While the development of the reservoir created vast expanses of farmland, supporting population and industrial growth in Taichung and Miaoli, the sacrifices were undeniable: the disappearance of villages and the stories of these lands. Only through inquiries from residents of Xinkai Village and those who migrated there, research into folk records, and official documents from the Hakka Affairs Council can we piece together a complete picture of the lives of the inhabitants of this land.

Chapter 02 · Ethnic CollisionChapter 02 · 族群碰撞

進一步分析這裡各個時期的族群分布，發現新開地區的歷史其實包含著客家人與巴宰族、泰雅族、日本人等。橫跨過乙未戰爭與太平洋戰爭，衍生出三段關鍵的民族發展歷程。

Further analysis of the distribution of ethnic groups reveals that Xinkai's history includes Hakka, Bazai, Atayal, and Japanese peoples. Spanning the Sino-Japanese and Pacific Wars, it evolved through three key stages of ethnic development.

The Challenge

水庫的發展導致田地損失、人口遷移及感情消逝。水面因為季節落差更高達 30 米，該如何借用這樣的時間關係讓水幫助重塑故土、活化地方成為課題。

Reservoir development led to loss of farmland, migration, and fading sentiment. With water levels fluctuating by 30 meters seasonally, the challenge is how to use this time relationship to let water reshape the land and revitalize the region.

Design ConceptDesign Concept · 設計概念

考量於本基地特殊的淹沒水文性質，以此處歷史最具意義的行易橋與永通橋為靈感，置入多個「橋」一般的裝置融入水位的自然起伏消長。不僅營造隨時間詮釋基地的紀念性景觀，也連結並活化新開村。

Considering the site's unique submerged hydrology, the design draws inspiration from the historically significant Xingyi and Yongtong Bridges. Multiple "bridge-like" structures are integrated to reflect natural water level fluctuations, creating a commemorative landscape that evolves over time and revitalizes Xinkai Village.

MAPPING THE MEMORY

透過衛星圖資的調查與在地耆老的訪問，得以還原出這片水下村落的全貌。作為故事發展的舞台，將透過水面上的「橋」，閱讀消失的蹤跡與故事。

Through satellite surveys and interviews with local elders, we reconstructed the full picture of this underwater village. As the stage for our story, the "bridges" on the water allow visitors to read the disappeared traces and their history.
